Mujra is a dance performance by females in a format that emerged in South Asia during the Mughal empire, where the elite class and local rulers like the nawabs of the Indian society (often connected to the Mughal emperor’s court) used to frequent courtesans for their entertainment at night. This trend was increasingly evident during the decadent or decline years of the Mughal empire.
