TUESDAY PUZZLE — As Trent Evans says in his notes below, puzzle people — solvers as well as those who make and edit them — are good people. But Mr. Evans is especially kind.

He’s a psychotherapist in his day job, and his constructor notes always seem to have an uplifting tenor to them. (As an aside, I have always loved the use of the word “tenor” to also mean “intent.” For all I know, an uplifting tenor could be a singer who bench-presses his own weight in sheet music, although I will confess that I know very little about opera.)

Anyway, Mr. Evans has some well wishes for us, and I highly recommend reading them, especially if you need a positive message right now. In addition, he offers links to his puzzle website and to Matt Gritzmacher’s daily crossword links round up, which delivers links to many puzzle sites in digest format.

And while we have seen this type of theme before, four of the entries made me smile and one made me laugh out loud, so good going, Mr. Evans. This was just what I needed today.

“Shimmering white stars set on an emerald sea”

Suffolk’s spinneys and copses are carpeted in wildflowers and the fragrance is intoxicating. Some will doubtless be bluebell woods, but around my home wild garlic is king. Ramsons crop up all over the place here, including in my garden, but there are two woods I can reach on foot where the broad, glossy straps of their leaves have been creating an unbroken emerald sea for weeks, and where now the white stars of their flowers glimmer among them, ethereal yet earthily pungent.

Woods such as these have long been landmarks, as evidenced by old place-names: Ramsbottom, Ramsholt, Ramsey, Ramshope. Really dense colonies take a great many decades to establish, and ramsons en masse can act as a clue to the age of a wood.

“It’s Office-Bound”

SATURDAY PUZZLE — I trust that everyone is enjoying a little extra puzzle time and staying productive, not getting stir crazy at all. Today we have a Peter Wentz construction, his first for 2020; it comes with 10 debuts and a generous sprinkle of fun clues and misdirects.

It’s probably just me, but there seems to be a trend lately on Saturdays where, instead of getting a block or two of span entries bound by lots of small fill, we get several mid-length words arranged in smaller (or staggered) stacks, which have a looser feel. While challenging, there’s a vivacity to this grid that I enjoy immensely.

Feel free to recommend any good archived puzzles in the comments, and please take care of yourselves!

Tricky Clues

For a grid with a lot of debuts there’s very little newspeak and branding today, and a lot of interesting bits and pieces. As far as misdirects, the southeast corner is tricky — I had “get serious” instead of SETTLE DOWN for a while and “beat it” for BUG OFF. The fun clues are absolutely fun but I look ASKANCE at DOES, which just seems too vague, and it took a little while to accept POD as clued. Seems as if there should be an “E” in there.
