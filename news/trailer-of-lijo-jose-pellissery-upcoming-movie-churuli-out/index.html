Trailer of Lijo Jose Pellissery's upcoming movie 'Churuli' out

Mollywood filmmaker Lijo Jose Pellissery, who recently announced that “from today onwards I am a Independent film maker. I will use all the money I raise from cinema to fuel better cinema and nothing else,” has come out with the trailer of his upcoming movie, Churuli.

The movie, which appears to be a thriller with elements of supernatural in it, features Pellissery regular Chemban Vinod Jose, Vinay Forrt, Joju George and Jaffer Idukki.

According to media reports, the shooting of the movie was completed along with his previous film Jallikkattu, which also featured Chemban Vinod Jose in the lead.

Earlier, Vinay Forrt had revealed that the shooting of the movie was completed in 19 days. The screenplay of Churuli is by popular Malayalam writer S. Hareesh.

The movie is produced by Lijo Jose Pellissery and Chemban Vinod Jose. The director had earlier said that he will screen his cinema anywhere he feels is right. “We are in the middle of a pandemic -a war-jobless people - identity crisis- poverty and religious unrest. People are walking a 1000 miles just to reach home. Artists are dying out of depression. So …these are times to create great art just to inspire people to feel alive. Just to give them hope in some form to stay alive,” he wrote in a Facebook post.

“Churuli trailer out: Lijo Jose Pellissery film is dark and intense”

Critically-acclaimed director Lijo Jose Pellissery has unveiled the trailer of his upcoming film, Churuli. On July 1, he shared the YouTube link to the trailer of Churuli, which seems like a dark and eerie tale.

The three-minute-long trailer of Churuli begins with animated visuals. A woman begins narrating a story about a phantom. Soon, we see actors Chemban Vinod, Vinay Forrt and a few others riding through a forest in a jeep.

These men are in search of a person named Mayiladanparambil Joy. Soon, the visuals get eerie with many spooky things happening. Churuli seems like a trademark Lijo Jose’s film with elements of mystery, humour and terrific performances.

Lijo Jose was in the news recently as he took to Facebook to express his thoughts on freedom of artistes to create content. The Malayalam producers’ association in Kerala had instructed directors not to begin shooting of new movies and asked them to wait for the pending ones to release.

Due to the shut down of theatres because of the novel coronavirus pandemic, more than 60 films couldn’t release. Lijo objected this decision and said that a director should be allowed to create content on his terms.

Churuli’s story is written by Vinoy Thomas, while its screenplay is by S Hareesh. According to reports, Churuli was shot in just 19 days.
