And they’re on! Fans and royals out in force at Cheltenham despite coronavirus fears

RACING fans undeterred by the coronavirus crisis turned out in force for the Cheltenham Festival opening yesterday. Joining the 60,664 spectators were the Queen’s granddaughter Zara Tindall, her husband Mike, and Princess Anne. Peter and Autumn Phillips, who recently announced their separation, put on a united front. Race organisers have installed extra wash basins and 300 hand sanitiser stations at the Gloucestershire course and said they were ‘very happy’, with ticket sales, down from a record 67,934 on opening day last year. Ian Renton, regional head of racing south-west for The Jockey Club, told BBC Breakfast: ‘We have liaised very closely with the government. They want business to continue as usual, so we have put a lot of precautions in place.’ Jockeys have been briefed to report ‘any symptoms straight away’, said Harry Cobden, who will ride Clan Des Obeaux in the Gold Cup on Friday. While some critics have dubbed the event a ‘ticking time bomb’ amid fears the virus will spread, racegoer Tom Hymas, of Barnsley, south Yorkshire, said: ‘If you keep washing your hands what more can you do?’
