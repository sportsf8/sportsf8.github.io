Coachella delayed over coronavirus ‘fears’

Coachella will now take place in October (Picture: Coachella, Getty Images) Coachella has officially been postponed until later this year due to the coronavirus outbreak. The annual music event had been scheduled to take place in April with Travis Scott, Rage Against The Machine and Frank Ocean headlining at the Indio, California site. But it has now been pushed back to October over health and safety concerns. Announcing the news in a statement, promoter GoldenVoice said on Tuesday: ‘At the direction of the County of Riverside and local health authorities, we must sadly confirm the rescheduling of Coachella and Stagecoach due to COVID-19 concerns. ‘While this decision comes at a time of universal uncertainty, we take the safety and health of our guests, staff and community very seriously. We urge everyone to follow the guidelines and protocols put forth by public health officials. ‘Coachella will now take place on October 9, 10 and 11 and October 16, 17 and 18, 2020.’ Travis Scott will grace Coachella’s main stage this autumn (Picture: Burak Cingi/Redferns) They added: ‘Stagecoach will take place on October 23, 24 and 25, 2020. All purchases for the April dates will be honoured for the rescheduled October dates. Purchasers will be notified by Friday, March 13 on how to obtain a refund if they are unable to attend. Advertisement Advertisement ‘Thank you for your continued support and we look forward to seeing you in the desert this fall.’ Frank Ocean is among the headliners (Picture: FilmMagic) It was originally set to take place across two weekends next month – 10-12 and 17-19 April. It comes after six cases of coronavirus were diagnosed in Riverside County, where Coachella takes place. Coachella will return in October (Picture: Getty Images) Conversations reportedly began on Sunday between talent agents and city officials about moving the festival, amid worries the permit for the festival would be pulled. Other artists slated to play Coachella include Lana Del Rey, Thom Yorke, BIGBANG, Megan Thee Stallion and 21 Savage, as well as UK acts including Calvin Harris, Lewis Capaldi, FKA twigs and Charli XCX. Coachella is a magnet for celebrity attendees, with stars including Kylie Jenner – who is in an on-off relationship with headliner – and Vanessa Hudgens staples at the desert festival. Stagecoach is due to be headlined by Carrie Underwood, Thomas Rhett and Eric Church. Coachella is just the latest in a growing line of festivals that have been forced to pull the plug on their upcoming events over the coronavirus outbreak. SXSW has also delayed their festival in Austin, while France’s Tomorrowland Winter, Ultra Abu Dhabi and Miami’s Ultra Music Festival are among the others to postpone.
