“Dani Speegle Dominates in Her Titan Games Debut”

It didn’t take long for Dani Speegle to make her presence known in her Titan Games debut on primetime television on Monday night. Billed as a “social-media star” she proved she was more than that and showed the world what the CrossFit community already knew, that she’s one of the fittest women on the planet.

Event 1, “Nuts and Bolts:” Speegle would take on NCAA women’s basketball referee Nadi Carey in her best-of-three showdown. Their match-up was the final for the women’s side of the Central Region. The women’s version of the event featured a wall with 1,200 lbs of weight that they had to remove before attempting to pull the wall down, releasing a lever. The first athlete to pull the lever wins.

Right from the get-go, Speegle showed her strength as she had no issue removing every plate from the wall while her competitor struggled. Speegle became the first athlete in the competition to remove every plate and the result was little effort used to pull the wall down. She pulled the lever to claim the victory. Speegle – 1, Carey – 0

Event 2, “Lunar Impact:” Starting on opposite ends of an elevated, crescent-shaped platform, each athlete had to scale a two-story ladder and then run atop the platform to a metal wall located at the middle and push against their opponent on the opposite side of the wall until one is forced off the platform.

Speegle beat Carey up the ladder and made it to the wall first and held the early advantage, coming within a foot of eliminating her. Carey rallied and pushed back at Speegle and turned the tide to her favor. Both athletes came to a standstill at the middle of the platform. Speegle found her second wind and slowly pushed back at Carey before finally pushing her off the structure for the victory and securing a chance to compete on Mount Olympus. Speegle – 2, Carey – 0

Mount Olympus: The final event featured the same structure from last week’s season premiere. Ten obstacles that test each athlete’s strength, speed, stamina and heart in a head-to-head race through the structure. Speegle took on the reigning Central Region Titan Chantae McMillan who won in her two trips to Mount Olympus.

McMillan, a two-time Olympian, held the advantage in both speed and familiarity with the event but Speegle kept even with her through the first three obstacles. McMillan made a costly error in the fourth obstacle, the “Log Lift”, giving Speegle a lead she would not relinquish. She had no issue with any of the remaining obstacles and showed her strength in the “Ball and Chain”, dragging the 250-lb wrecking ball with ease and smashing her way through the “Titan Tomb” with her sledgehammer to secure the key and turn the lock giving her the title of Titan

What’s next: Speegle takes over the mantle as Central Region Champion and now advances to next week’s Regional finals to defend her title once again on Mount Olympus against other Regional competitors before moving onto the final..

In the men’s competition, former NFL great Joe Thomas continued his dominance over all his challengers as he once again defended his Regional Titan title by defeating sports medicine physician Kaleb Redden on Mount Olympus

“Paddler Mudit Dani serves it right on Instagram”

At a time when physical interactions have become a strict no-no, the virtual world has come to the rescue of many of us, and sportspersons are no different. Many famous names from the sporting community have gone live on various social media platforms, interacting with fellow players, fans, etc. Among them is upcoming table tennis player Mudit Dani , who won bronze medal at the US Open in December last year.From his Mumbai home, Dani has been talking to some of India’s most successful sportspersons, both former and current, on Instagram Live to find inspiration during these difficult times through their stories of struggle and triumph.“I learnt some important things. One of them is focusing on the process and yourself rather than the results because it (your achievement or the lack of it) is the result of what you do in training. And if you can’t do things you want to do in the training, there is no way you can do those things in the match,” the 21-year-old Dani told ET Sport.Dani, who became the sixth highest-ranked Indian after breaking into the top-200 of the world rankings recently, is not the only sportsperson interviewing sports stars on social media though. In fact, he is among the least popular. You have the likes of Rohit Sharma , Ravichandran Ashwin, Jemimah Rodrigues and Smriti Mandhana, and Sunil Chhetri doing similar sessions.But the list of people who have been on Dani’s show — In the Sportlight — is quiet impressive for a 21-year-old who is yet to make a mark at the highest stage. So far, he has had Abhinav Bindra (shooting, 2008 Olympic gold medallist), Leander Paes (tennis, 1996 Olympics bronze medallist and multiple grand slam winner), G Sathiyan (table tennis, 2018 CWG gold medallist, 2018 Asian Games bronze medallist), Manika Batra (table tennis, 2018 CWG gold medallist, 2018 Asian Games bronze medallist), Dipika Pallikal (squash, 2018 CWG gold medallist), Apurvi Chandela (shooting, 2014 CWG gold medallist, 2018 Asian Games bronze medallist), Anirudh Thapa (football, Indian national team, Chennaiyin FC) and Parupalli Kashyap (badminton, 2014 CWG gold medallist) on his show. And he is set to host sports minister Kiren Rijiju on his show next week (Friday, June 12).The idea of starting this interview-based show, he said, came after receiving a lot of questions on his Instagram handle about his routine during the lockdown. Being on the circuit for more than 10 years has helped Dani build a ‘connect’ with many athletes.“I thought if I was able to connect them to many of the people who have been asking me, there will be a better platform for everyone to understand what every athlete is going through right now, what they are doing to improve their game and mental space,” said Dani.The initial plan was to talk to just 6-7 of his close table tennis friends, but then one thing led to another and he ended up having such big names for his show.“So, I reached out to a couple of people and they connected me to a few more people. …then it got really big. I then cut on the TT thing and made it multi-sport where I could reach out to any possible Indian. If Abhinav Bindra and Leader Paes are talking, anyone would want to listen,” he said.Unlike many other sportspersons who are doing similar shows, Dani has no team to help him prepare. His allies are his family members. “I do end up consulting my sister and parents. Sometimes I also ask my friends,” he said, adding that Google comes in handy to source information about his guests.Interestingly, there has been no cricketer on the show so far. Dani is not particularly averse to the idea, but he wants to have only the top names. “If I have an Abhinav Bindra and a Leander Pae, I would need a Rohit Sharma or a Virat Kohli or somebody of that stature,” he said.As of now, Indian hockey player Yuvraj Walmiki and woman footballer Dalima Chhibber are in line to appear on his show. Though he isn’t sure for how long he will be able to continue the show, as the players have started returning to training, Dani has already planned the final.“Sharath (Kamal) is going to be the last episode. I decided that in the beginning only. I wanted to start with TT, so I did my first episode with Sathiyan, and (I am going to) end with Sharath,” he said.Dani has the distinction of being only the third Indian player after Soumyajit Ghosh and Harmeet Desai to break into the top-10 of the International Table Tennis Federation (ITTF) World Junior Boys’ Circuit standings. He continued his good work even after joining the senior circuit in 2018. Within two years, he has moved from being outside of top-700 to top-200 in the world rankings.But this long break from competition and training because of the suspension of all ITTF events can break his momentum. Is he worried?“There is no reason to think much about it. The situation is what it is and nobody can change it,” he said. “In a way, it’s actually a boon that I left off on a positive note.”While at home, Dani understands he has to keep himself physically and mentally ready so that when the training restarts, he doesn’t have to start from scratch. In consultation with his trainer Yogesh Kanchgar and coach Kamlesh Mehta, he has been working on his fitness every day for around two hours that includes exercises for strength, mobility and stretching. He is also talking regularly with his sports psychologist Mugdha Bavare to mentally prepare himself for the grind.With the help of his coach, he has also managed to source a table from a local club recently. “Obviously, I don’t have anyone to play with, but I am able to serve at least,” he said.
