The Royals Show Coronavirus Caution and Carry On at Westminster Abbey

As coronavirus precautions occupied people around the world, a surprisingly detailed hand-washing instructional manual released by the U.K.’s National Health Service became the source material for a meme over the weekend. And even as American government’s response remains disorganized, Prince Charles used Monday’s Commonwealth Day service at Westminster Abbey to show off more British skills for avoiding infection. Instead of shaking hands as he greeted the representatives of the Commonwealth of Nations, he pressed his palms together and gave a slight bow, performing the customary Indian greeting. Meghan Markle and Prince Harry held each other's hands when they arrived, but Prince William and Kate Middleton did not when they followed just a few minutes after. Seated in different rows during the service, the younger royals also seemingly avoided physical contact, and merely waved at each other and mouthed “hello” upon arrival. (Coronavirus caution or ongoing feud? Who's to say.) As Meghan and Harry left, they stopped outside to greet schoolchildren, and she even crouched down to speak with a young girl in a Girl Guides uniform. While Charles came up with a creative way to acknowledge the moment, all of the royal family refrained from shaking hands during the service. According to the Daily Mail’s Rebecca English, this was a Westminster Abbey–wide mandate. Currently, there have been 319 cases of the novel coronavirus in the U.K., the BBC reports, and three of those who tested positive have died. Charles’s spokesperson at Clarence House told the Sun that the prince and his wife, Camilla, Duchess of Cornwall, both intended to keep up their royal duties despite the threat of the virus, including a previously announced 10-day trip to Bosnia and Jordan. “We are following government advice,” the spokesperson added. At an event in Dublin last week, Prince William cracked a joke about how he and Kate Middleton might be spreading the virus to Ireland, which had only one confirmed case at the time, though more have since been announced. Later on, a spokesperson for the couple told People that the couple has been taking the virus seriously, and has been conducting business as usual on the advice of Public Health England.
