Liverpool have suffered drastically over the course of the past decade due to poor goalkeeping, which changed significantly once Alisson Becker moved to Merseyside.

In 2017/18, for example, Liverpool’s xG for and against was notably better than Manchester United’s and the Reds also exceeded their rivals according to various other performance indicators.

However, Jose Mourinho’s outfit finished second in the table while Jurgen Klopp’s men placed fourth, and much of that was due to how David De Gea’s shot-stopping ability secured results for United that seemed undeserved.

The Spanish international was expected to concede around 43.5 goals, but he shipped only 28.

Goalkeepers are prone to taking a back seat when their team is performing well, but they are crucial when competing for the most prestigious honours.

Regardless of the strength of Chelsea’s attack - if they are willing to persist with deficiencies in goal, they will suffer from sporadic moments and their results will continue to fluctuate more so than a title-winning side can afford.

Lampard’s men are almost certainly set to improve but until they resolve their goalkeeping problem, they shouldn’t be considered as a legitimate threat to Liverpool’s dominance.
