CNN anchor Chris Cuomo has once again gone from reporting the news to being in the news, this time after allegedly getting caught naked in his wife’s yoga video on Instagram.

Some of Cristina Greeven Cuomo’s 123,000 Instagram followers tuned in late May to watch the mother-of-three wellness guru practice yoga at her home studio, only to get an eyeful of a man’s nude backside outside her window overlooking the yard.

The video shot on or around May 27 was almost immediately removed from Instagram Live, but not before some quick-thinking users got screenshots of the man in his birthday suit and shared them on social media, as Page Six first reported.

The images show an athletic-looking man from behind, with his exposed physique partially blocked by a column. The screengrabs from the now-deleted video do not show the photo-bombing culprit’s face.

As of Monday afternoon, neither Chris Cuomo nor his wife have addressed the cheeky video.

New York Governor Andrew Cuomo’s younger brother has been broadcasting his CNN show, Cuomo Prime Time, from the basement of his family’s sprawling mansion in Southampton, New York, since the beginning of the coronavirus pandemic.

In late March, the 49-year-old Chris announced on his show that he had tested positive for COVID-19, which left him shivering from high fever and hallucinating. His wife and their 14-year-old son, Mario, later contracted the illness as well.

Cristina, 50, a self-described ‘wellness worshiper,’ has been sharing videos of herself practicing yoga in her home studio overlooking the garden

After spending several weeks quarantined in the basement, in mid-April the TV presenter filmed himself reuniting with his family.

‘This is the dream. Just to be back up here doing normal things,’ he said as he embraced his wife.

But Cuomo was accused of staging the clip after he revealed he left his home on Easter Sunday - two weeks after testing positive - and confronted a ‘fat biker’ who asked him why he wasn’t in quarantine.

In late April, the CNN host announced he was coronavirus-free, but as recently as last month he admitted that he was still not ‘100 per cent.’

‘I still have weird stuff going on with my lungs,’ Cuomo told his viewers.

At the height of the COVID-19 outbreak in New York City, Cristina, 50, a self-described ‘wellness worshiper,’ sparked a controversy when she recommended on her blog, The Purist, bathing in one half of a cup of Clorox bleach twice a week to boost her immune system.

Both Chris and Cristina battled the coronavirus in March. In Mid-April, the CNN host filmed himself reuniting with his wife (pictured)

She later defended her use of bleach in an interview with People Magazine, saying in part: ‘there’s a huge opposition against holistic medicine, I get that. We are all trying to find tools to help beat this.’

In the now deleted blog post Cristina incorrectly said that the bleach is ‘technically salt’ and claimed she used it to ‘combat the radiation and metals in my system’, in direct contrast to warnings given by Clorox that contact with skin should be avoided.

The blogger also wrote about boosting immunity to the virus through a $300 vitamin C intravenous drip.

For the past month, Cristina has been sharing videos with her 123,000 Instagram followers showing herself practicing yoga.

In her most recent video, uploaded on Sunday, the windows of Cristina’s studio were shielded with blinds.

En la captura, aparentemente, se ve al hermano del gobernador de Nueva York, Andrew Cuomo, muy relajado en el exterior de su casa, donde había estado transmitiendo su programa mientras se recuperaba tras contraer COVID-19.

Sin embargo, Cuomo, padre de tres hijos, no ha sido nada tímido con la exposición pública. El 31 de marzo comunicó en sus redes que había dado positivo por coronavirus, y contó en su programa de primetime su dolorosa batalla para superarlo.

Cuomo estuvo refugiado en Southampton durante los meses que luchaba contra el coronavirus, pero recientemente fue noticia por un altercado en público. Una persona nada contenta con verlo en la calle, sabiendo que había estado enfermo, se acercó a increparlo. El presentador contó la historia en su programa SiriusXM y se mostró molesto por cómo la fama había impactado en su vida privada. “No quiero que un motociclista gordo y perdedor pueda detenerse y hablar tonterías conmigo, no quiero escucharlo”, dijo Cuomo en su programa.

La esposa del periodista también había estado involucrada en una controversia recientemente cuando describió los pasos que tomó para ayudar a su esposo para que se recupere de COVID-19. La instructora de yoga, que luchó contra un caso leve del virus, le dio a su esposo baños de cloro, medicinas herbales y un goteo de vitamina C de USD 300, informó la revista People. La mujer detalló sus métodos en una publicación de su blog, que recibió críticas inmediatas por compartir alimentos y tratamientos homeopáticos que no tienen respaldo científico.
