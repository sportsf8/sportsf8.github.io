New Delhi | Jagran News Desk: Chaitra Navratri is an auspicious Hindu festival, dedicated to goddess Durga, and celebrated across the country for nine days. During this festival, different forms of the goddess are worshipped by the devotees. The festival commemorates with Kalasthapna and concludes on Ramnavami. Navratri is celebrated to mark the victory of good over evil.

During this festival, the ardent devotees of Maa Durga observe fast across nine days and remain awake at night for bhajan and keertan. This year, Chaitra Navratri will begin on March 25 and conclude on April 2.

As per legends, goddess Durga killed evil Mahisasura– a demon who attacked Trilok (Earth, Heaven and Hell)– by taking several forms during this period (Navratri). Navratri is celebrated twice every year, i.e., during the month of March/April and October/November. While the former is known as Chaitra Navratri, the other is simply referred to as Navratri.
