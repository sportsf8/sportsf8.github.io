Gonzalo Rodríguez just called it quits on his brilliant career

An all-time Fiorentina great just hung up his boots. “The young defender” Gonzalo Rodríguez, now 36, explained in an interview with current club San Lorenzo that he’s ready to call it quits and spend more time with his family and friends. He’d returned to his boyhood outfit San Lorenzo after a lamentable departure from Fiorentina in 2017, when the Della Valle brothers and Pantaleo Corvino refused to extend his contract.

I can list out the numbers for you, but they won’t do Bomber Gonzalo any sort of justice, even though they’re deeply impressive in their own right. Bought from Villarreal for just €1.5 million in 2012 after the Yellow Submarine sank into relegation with Borja Valero and Giuseppe Rossi, the defender arrived with a reputation for intelligent and physical play matched only by a propensity for injury, with cruciate tears and a broken fibula (!) marring his time at the Cerámica.

“In Florence, I lived five very emotional years. When I arrived from Villarreal, I didn’t expect it to be like this. When I was chosen I couldn’t believe I was alongside Dunga and Baggio.” —Gonzalo

We expected him to start right away and he did. We didn’t expect him to stay on the pitch forever, but he missed just 10 games through injury in 5 years. For that half decade, he piled up 17,886 minutes across 203 appearances, an eye-popping 25 goals, 6 assists, 66 yellow cards, and 7 red cards.

He’s got the 15th most appearances for a Viola defender in club history, and his 25 goals trail just countryman and all-world star Daniel Passarella and flying fullback Sergio Cervato. He trails just Gabriel Batistuta in Fiorentina appearances for an Argentine. He wore the armband for two years. That he only earned 7 caps for his country while guys like José Basanta and Martín Demichelis were regulars points to the glaring favoritism (or corruption, if we’re being a bit more honest) that scuppers Argentina internationally at every tournament.

The numbers aren’t even close to doing Gonzalo justice, though, because it was his personality and the moments of wonder he provided that really explain his impact. In those dazzling Vincenzo Montella sides based around slick passing through the middle and technically marvelous players getting up the pitch, Gonzalo added a dimension of long passing out from the back, sure, but he also added a toughness that was otherwise wholly lacking. He wasn’t afraid to body up bigger players or throw himself around to kill off the counterattacks that sprang up whenever Fiorentina lost the ball. If Valero was the beating heart of those sides, Gonzalo was their fearless and expansive soul.

I can’t describe any more of what made him so special on the pitch, so here’s a woefully inadequate 10 minute video of thunderous and precise tackles, out-of-nowhere clearances, laser-guided 60 meter passes, and, of course, the goals.

A slightly introverted figure, he was never the sort to invite the fans into his life like il Sindacco did. Gonzalo, though, connected with the community in ways that no other player did. He settled down and had a kid with local gelateria owner Paula Martina Alessi, with whom he seems to have a genuinely beautiful relationship. He stepped on stage and played guitar in local bars. He was just so effortlessly cool.

After Corvino and the Della Valles refused to extend his contract, he gracefully moved back to San Lorenzo and has been solid there for the past two years, mostly working as a mentor off the pitch but still more than able to come in and do a job, making 55 appearances and adding another 3 goals.

We’re as thrilled that he gets to end his career on his own terms as we were dismayed that he was forced out of Florence. He’s earned the right to do whatever he wants now, but we really hope that he’ll be back in Tuscany as at least a frequent and regular visitor, if not a full-time resident. There are so many Fiorentina supporters who love him, and we’re part of that group. It’d be brilliant to see him return, either in some capacity with the club or as a private citizen.

No matter what, though, congratulations on a brilliant career, Gonzalo, and thank you for everything.

“Gonzalo Rodríguez le puso punto final a su carrera”

“Gonzalo Rodríguez’s best San Lorenzo moments”

Gonzalo Rodríguez began his career at San Lorenzo, making his debut in 2002. He went on to play in Europe for Villarreal and Fiorentina, before returning to San Lorenzo in 2017. The Argentinian defender announced his retirement in June 2020.

“L’addio al calcio di Gonzalo Rodriguez, ex capitano della Fiorentina”

“E’ un momento molto delicato per me. A venti anni ho lasciato la mia famiglia, e adesso la lascio nuovamente”. Gonzalo Javier Rodriguez, ex difensore e capitano anche della Fiorentina, lascia il calcio giocato. Lo fa con un annuncio in diretta su Instagram, tramite l’account della squadra del San Lorenzo, dove ha deciso di concludere la sua carriera a 36 anni.In viola dal 2012 al 2017 ha rappresentato uno dei punti fermi del rilancio dell’ultima gestione dei Della Valle. “Ringrazio tutti i club in cui ho giocato, tra cui anche la Fiorentina - continua l’argentino - furono cinque anni di grandissima emozione. La gente di Firenze mi ama, mi rispetta per tutto quello che ho dato in campo. Non pensavo di passare momenti così belli a Firenze. Quando mi sono trasferito dal Villareal alla Fiorentina non potevo credere di essere premiato accanto a Dunga a Baggio. C'è tantissima gente che dedica il proprio tempo e la propria passione al San Lorenzo. Saluto tutti i miei compagni, mi dispiace ora non poterli vedere personalmente per salutarli ma sicuramente quando ci sarà la possibilità di vederci sfrutteremo l’occasione per farlo”.

Gonzalo si è sempre distinto per il suo carattere da leader. Fascia al braccio, trascinatore vero e amato dai fiorentini per la sua grinta che trasmetteva giocatori e tifosi. A fine stagione, nel 2017, non gli fu rinnovato il contratto dall’allora direttore generale Pantaleo Corvino. “Non so cosa farò - continua Gonzalo - Ho girato molte squadra ma alla Fiorentina sapevano che sarei voluto tornare qua per concludere la mia carriera. Sono orgoglioso anche se è arrivato il momento di dire addio al calcio. Avrei voluto giocare molto di più dopo il mio ritorno con la maglia del San Lorenzo. Lascio con grande tristezza questo lavoro dopo trent’anni. Però al tempo stesso c'è ora la voglia di stare con la mia famiglia, coi miei amici. Si apre una nuova tappa della mia vita. Mi piacerebbe molto rimanere in questo club”.Gonzalo Rodriguez ha spostato Martina Alessi, conosciuta proprio ai tempi di Firenze, dalla quale ha avuto due bambini

“Gonzalo Rodríguez, la desgracia con las lesiones y sus críticas al fútbol argentino”

Gonzalo Rodríguez señaló como una de sus cuentas pendientes no haber podido jugar un Mundial y, si bien no se reprochó nada, consideró que las lesiones no lo ayudaron para lograr ese objetivo.

“Fue una espina quizás. Siempre cuando estaba a punto, me lesionaba. Un mes antes del 2006 podría haber ido y me lastimé el tobillo. Cuando regreso, me convoca el Coco Basile y ese día me rompo los ligamentos cruzados. Se me fueron poniendo piedras en el camino y eso hizo que no aproveche la oportunidad”, afirmó.

Por último, hizo una crítica a la organización del fútbol argentino: “Tiene que haber un equilibrio que venga desde arriba. Un campeonato cambia el descenso, después el formato del torneo… En cambio, en Europa es todo mucho más fácil y organizado”.

“Social – Borja Valero omaggia Gonzalo Rodriguez: l’ex Fiorentina si ritira”

236 partite disputate dallo stesso lato del campo, con le maglie di Villareal e Fiorentina, non si scordano facilmente. Così, Borja Valero ha voluto omaggiare con un post su Instagram il suo “fratello calcistico” Gonzalo Rodriguez, che nella giornata odierna ha annunciato il suo ritiro dal mondo del pallone.

Ecco l’omaggio dell’attuale centrocampista nerazzurro: “Lascia il calcio un autentico crack; è stato un piacere godere della tua qualità. Buona fortuna per tutto ciò che ti accadrà“.
