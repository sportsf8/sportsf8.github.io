Giorgio Armani Has a Prediction About the Future of Design

"Getting dressed and decorating are similar activities, but with an important difference," Giorgio Armani tells AD PRO. "Clothing changes by definition, and getting dressed means expressing oneself in different ways every day. Home décor, on the other hand, remains the same for much longer." Armani's words are as true as they are insightful. But with the opening of the new Miami Residences by Armani/Casa, they're also more relevant than ever. This past Friday, the building was fêted with a multi-hour bash-slash-fashion show where the brand's Spring 2020 collection was presented. Parties aside, there's a lot to unpack for interior design aficionados when it comes to the structure itself. The residences and common areas feature Armani furniture, but the building itself is interestingly one of the final designs created by the late César Pelli, who died this past summer at age 92. Located on Sunny Isles Beach, the palatial high-rise features state-of-the-art interior details as well, and appliances by Sub-Zero and Wolf. A lounge area in the new Residences by Armani/Casa. Photo: Courtesy of Residences by Armani/Casa The spa features mint green club style chairs. Photo: Courtesy of Residences by Armani/Casa "The residences themselves are an interior designers’ dream—they feature expansive floor-plans and floor-to-ceiling windows that make you feel as though you’re floating above the ocean," Jon Paul Pérez, one of the partners of the project and an executive vice president of The Related Group, tells AD PRO. Gil Dezer, the president of Dezer Development, which also worked on the project, adds that sales for the project are strong so far, with the building being "well on its way" to meeting its planned $1 billion sellout. "Finally seeing the project come to fruition, along with the community’s receptiveness so far, is an extremely proud moment for all teams involved," he reflects to AD PRO. On Friday night, Armani celebrated the new residences with a multi-hour event. The evening, which included a fashion show, featured fireworks. "I enjoy fashion and design, because in both worlds I can express my aesthetic vision and my style," he says. "They are both expressions of the human spirit." Nevertheless, with the relationship that exists between fashion and design only seeming to strengthen in recent years, there are undoubtedly changes in store for the 2020s and the rest of the 21st century. As a pioneering figure within the spheres of both fashion and design, Armani is in a particularly interesting position to make some predictions: "In the future I envision more fluid, modular solutions, ones that can be reconfigured more easily," he says. Any time Mr. Armani shares his well-honed insights, we'd be wise to listen.
