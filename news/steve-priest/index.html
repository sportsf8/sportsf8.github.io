Family did not share a cause of death for the glam rock star, who had 13 Top 20 hits in the 1970s

Steve Priest, bassist and vocalist with glam rock band The Sweet, has died age 72. His bandmates confirmed the news, sharing a statement from the family. The cause of death is not yet known.

Bandmate Andy Scott paid tribute to Priest, describing him as the best bass player he had ever played with. “From that moment in the summer of 1970 when we set off on our musical odyssey the world opened up and the roller coaster ride started.”

David Ellefson of Megadeth said that Priest was “without parallel”. He said that The Sweet “gave me one of my earliest memories of great hard rock on the radio as a kid and [1974’s] Desolation Boulevard still holds up as one of rock’s greatest albums from that period.”

The Sweet were key figures in the glam rock era, scoring 13 Top 20 hits in the 1970s. Priest joined the fledgling group, then known as The Sweetshop, in the late 60s. After a false start on Parlophone at the turn of the 70s, they signed to RCA worldwide and had their first hit with Funny Funny in 1971.

It would take them a year to repeat that kind of impact, finally igniting a run of chart success with the singles Little Willy and Wig-Wam Bam. They left behind their early bubblegum aesthetic to embrace the outre stylings of the budding glam movement, often to hostile response from fans unfamiliar with men in makeup.

But Priest soon became known and beloved by fans for his playful demeanour. He became “the instigator of androgynous mischief”, wrote Simon Reynolds in Shock and Awe: Glam Rock and Its Legacy. “Milky skin, wavy red hair, puckered lips… ‘I looked like a 12-year-old girl’, Priest later recalled.

He got in hot water when he appeared in German military uniform and sporting a Hitler-style moustache on the 1973 Christmas edition of Top of the Pops. “It’s amazing how everyone still talks about the Nazi uniform,” he said in 2010. “Good old BBC wardrobe department. People always want to know if I was serious. I mean, a gay Hitler. Hello?!”

The group enjoyed the spoils of success. “If it breathed and was female, it was fair game,” Priest told the Guardian. “The 70s were magical. They were like the 60s, only crazier. God knows how we got away with it.”

Towards the middle of the decade, they embraced a harder sound – one unintentionally entrenched after frontman Brian Connolly sustained a throat wound in a fight in Staines and emerged with a rougher singing style. His confidence shattered, he began drinking heavily.

His injuries meant the band had to turn down a personal invitation to support their heroes The Who at Charlton Athletic football ground in May 1974, which they believed would have transformed their reputation from “a superficial glam machine” to “serious rock contenders”, said critic David Cavanaugh.

After Connolly departed the group in 1978, Priest took over lead vocal duties for the remainder of the group’s first incarnation. They disbanded in 1981, having taken a year off after drummer Mick Tucker’s wife drowned in the bath in 1979.

Over subsequent decades, the group would reunite regularly in a number of conflicting incarnations: Priest had the right to use the band’s name in the US and Canada, whereas guitarist Andy Scott toured a version of the group in the UK. The pair were estranged for decades, but resumed contact in the 2010s. Scott said the pair mooted a reunion for the band’s 50th anniversary, although this never came to pass.

Priest was born in Hayes, Middlesex, on 23 February 1948. He is survived by his wife, Maureen, whom he married in 1981 as the band was approaching disbandment, and their three daughters. Maureen, née O’Connor, was a director of publicity for Capital and EMI in New York.

“Steve Priest, Bassist for The Sweet, Dead at 72”

Steve Priest, bassist for the glam rock band The Sweet, has died. He was 72.

His passing was confirmed by The Sweet bandmate guitarist Andy Scott via the band’s official Facebook page.

“Then there was one,” began Scott’s post, referencing late original vocalist Brian Connolly and drummer Mick Tucker. “I am in pieces right now. Steve Priest has passed away. His wife Maureen and I have kept in contact and though his health was failing I never envisaged this moment. Never. My thoughts are with his family.”

Scott continued, “He was the best bass player I ever played with. The noise we made as a band was so powerful. From that moment in the summer of 1970 when set off on our Musical Odyssey the world opened up and the rollercoaster ride started! He eventually followed his heart and moved to the USA. First New York then LA. Rest in Peace brother. All my love.”

The Sweet released nine studio albums from 1971-1982. Their most successful album, Desolation Boulevard, featured some of their biggest songs, including “Ballroom Blitz,” “Fox on the Run” and “A.C.D.C.” Other popular songs from The Sweet include “Wig-Wam Bam,” “Little Willy,” “Teenage Rampage,” “Blockbuster” and “Action.”

“The Sweet bassist Steve Priest dies at 72”

The Sweet co-founder Steve Priest has died at the age of 72.

The bass player and singer’s longtime bandmate, Andy Scott, confirmed the sad news via Facebook on Thursday, confessing he was struggling to come to terms with his friend’s death.

“I am in pieces right now,” Scott wrote. “Steve Priest has passed away. His wife Maureen and I have kept in contact, and though his health was failing, I never envisaged this moment.”

Calling Priest “the best bass player I ever played with”, Scott added: “The noise we made as a band was so powerful. From that moment in the summer of 1970 when set off on our musical odyssey, the world opened up and the roller-coaster ride started!”

The Sweet became a glam-rock supergroup thanks to hits like Ballroom Blitz and Blockbuster, before disbanding in 1982.

Priest declined to rejoin the group years later when Scott staged a reunion, but the bassist put together his own The Sweet revival in 2008, touring across the U.S.

He chronicled his life and his career as a member of The Sweet in the 1994 book, Are You Ready, Steve?

Priest ajudou a fundar o Sweet em 1968 atuando como baixista. De 1979 a 1982 ele também se tornou o vocalista principal dessa importante banda de glam rock.

Ledende band innen glamrock

Priest var en av originalmedlemmene i bandet og begynte å spille gitar på 60-tallet. Sweet ble grunnlagt i London i 1968.

I løpet av 70-tallet ble de et av de ledende bandene innen såkalt glamrock med sanger som «Little Willy», «Wig Wam Bam», «Ballroom Blitz» og «Block Buster!».

I midten av karrieren gikk bandet over til hardrock, med singler som «Turn It Down». Bandet samarbeidet med låtskriverne Mike Chapman (73) og Nicky Chinn (75), som skrev flere av bandets mest kjente sanger.

I 1982 ble bandet oppløst, men «gjenoppsto» i flere forskjellige versjoner på 1980- og 1990-tallet. I dag eksisterer bandet i to versjoner, Steve Priests Sweet og Andy Scotts Sweet.

Glamrockaren Steve Priest död – blev 72 år

Var en av originalmedlemmarna i Sweet

Steve Priest är död.

Sweets basist och sångare blev 72 år gammal, rapporterar Mirror.

”Det är med ett tungt hjärta som vi meddelar att Steve Priest är död”, skriver hans familj i ett uttalande.

”Are you ready Steve?”

Den meningen känner säkert många igen från dunderhiten ”The Ballroom Blitz” med rockbandet Sweet. Nu är Steve Priest död, rapporterar bland annat Mirror.

Han var en av originalmedlemmarna i bandet och började spela bas under 60-talet för att sedan även sjunga under senare år.

Sörjs av familjen

Han dog under torsdagen och sörjs av sin familj.

”Det är med ett tungt hjärta som vi meddelar att Steve Priest är död. Han efterlämnar sig sin fru Maureen, tre döttrar, Lisa, Danielle och Maggie och sina tre barnbarn Jordan, Jade och Hazel”, skriver hans familj i ett uttalande.

Bandet grundades i London 1968 och under 70-talet släppte de bland annat superhiten ”The Ballrom Blitz” som fick enormt stor spridning världen över. De toppade även listorna under denna tid.

”Vi är väldigt ledsna men vi uppskattar också er lojalitet”

När hyllningarna började strömma in från fansen valde bandet att lägga upp ett inlägg och tacka för stödet.

”Tack till alla för de fina orden. Vi är väldigt ledsna men vi uppskattar också er lojalitet och vänlighet under många år. Vi kanske inte kommer kunna svara alla individuellt, men Steves familj och bandet är medvetna om och uppskattar er support.”

Förutom ”The Ballroom Blitz” har bandet bland annat gjort låtar som ”Fox on The Run”, ”Action”, ”Teenage Rampage”, ”Hellraiser”, ”Blockbuster”, ”The Six Teens”, ”Love is Like Oxygen”, ”Turn it Down”, ”Wig-Wam Bam” och Little Willy.

“Muusikko Steve Priest, 72, on kuollut”

1970-luvulla suosiota niittäneen The Sweet -yhtyeen basistia jää kaipaamaan tämä vaimo, kolme lasta ja kolme lapsenlasta.

Steve Priest esiintymässä The Pavilionissa vuonna 1973. Ian Dickson/Shutterstock/All Over Press

The Sweet - yhtyeen basisti Steve Priest on kuollut 72 - vuotiaana . Asiasta ilmoitti yhtyeen kitaristi Andy Scott yhtyeen Facebook - sivulla.

– Olen musertunut juuri nyt . Steve Priest on menehtynyt . Olen ollut yhteydessä hänen vaimonsa Maureenin kanssa, ja vaikka hänen terveytensä oli heikentymässä, en koskaan osannut kuvitella tätä hetkeä . En ikinä . Ajatukseni ovat hänen perheensä luona, Scott kirjoittaa .

The Sweet perustettiin Lontoossa vuonna 1968 . Yhtye niitti menestystä 1970 - luvulla, jolloin 13 bändin kappaletta päätyi top 20 - listoille . Hittibiisi ”Blockbuster” nousi Britanniassa listaykköseksi vuonna 1973 .

Basson lisäksi Priest vaikutti useilla levytyksillä taustalaulajana . Hänet tunnettiin etenkin korkeasta äänestään . Brian Connellyn jätettyä bändi vuonna 1979, Priestistä tuli The Sweetin laulaja . Yhtye hajosi vuonna 1982 .

Vuonna 2008 Priest perusti oman The Sweet - yhtyeensä Amerikan - kiertuetta varten, vaikka Scottin johtama virallinen Sweet oli edelleen toiminnassa .

– Hän oli paras basisti, jonka kanssa olen soittanut . Meteli, jota pidimme bändissä oli niin voimakasta, Scott kirjoittaa .

– Lepää rauhassa, veli . Kaikki rakkauteni, Scott päättää lausuntonsa .
